Cookies Policy

Introduction to Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device to help websites remember you and your preferences, improve functionality, and deliver personalized content. They have become an essential part of the modern internet, providing seamless and richer experiences for millions of users worldwide.

As the internet and data privacy regulations have evolved, understanding how cookies work and the control you have over them is critical. This Cookies Policy explains in detail how Ever Green Gardening uses cookies, your choices, and how to manage cookie settings effectively.

Cookies come in various forms and serve distinct purposes; appreciating their roles helps you make informed decisions while browsing our site or using our services.

Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential to operate our website’s core functions. They enable security protocols, login sessions, shopping carts, and form submissions. Without these cookies, certain services or features cannot be provided.

Strictly necessary cookies are exempt from consent requirements under regulatory frameworks as they solely ensure basic functionality.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

We use these cookies to collect aggregated and anonymous data about the way visitors interact with our site. This information allows us to better understand usage patterns, optimize content layout, and ensure efficient operations.

Analytics providers, such as Google Analytics, may set these cookies on our behalf. Individual user identification is not possible through this data.

Functional Cookies

These cookies store information about choices you make, such as language preferences, themes, or login settings, to enhance and personalize your user experience.

Functional cookies improve usability but are not essential for basic site operation.

Targeting or Advertising Cookies

These cookies track your browsing habits to deliver relevant advertisements that align with your interests. They may also limit the frequency of ads and measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.

Third parties usually administer targeting cookies and are responsible for their privacy practices.

Session vs Persistent Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and expire once you close your browser, whereas persistent cookies remain on your device for a predetermined period or until manually deleted.

The duration and scope of cookies depend on their purpose and regulatory requirements.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies serve multiple functions enabling us to:

  • Maintain your login and session state for smoother navigation.
  • Remember your preferences, such as region or language.
  • Analyze usage data to improve website design and content.
  • Customize offers and services based on browsing behavior.
  • Protect our website and users from fraudulent activity.
  • Manage your cookie consent preferences efficiently.

This usage ensures a tailored and secure experience while complying with applicable data protection legislations.

Managing and Controlling Cookies

You have control over cookie preferences using browser settings. You may:

  • Block or restrict cookies selectively or completely.
  • Delete cookies stored on your device at any time.
  • Set preferences to receive notifications before cookies are stored.

Note that blocking or removing essential cookies may impair website functionality, including the inability to log in or save your preferences.

For detailed instructions on managing cookies, refer to your browser's help documentation or visit resources like aboutcookies.org.

Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies may be set by external services embedded in our website for advertising, analytics, or interactive features (e.g., social media feeds).

While we strive to work with reputable partners, we have no direct control over their practices. Their privacy policies govern how they use data collected through these cookies.

We encourage you to review these policies to understand and manage your privacy settings effectively.

Cookies and Privacy Laws

Our use of cookies complies with international privacy regulations, including but not limited to:

  • GDPR: European Union regulation requiring clear consent for non-essential cookies and enhanced transparency about data processing.
  • CCPA: California law granting consumers rights over personal data including cookie-derived information.
  • ePrivacy Directive: EU directive governing electronic communications and requiring cookie consent.

These frameworks influence our policies and consent mechanisms, ensuring you have control over how your data is used.

You may modify your cookie preferences or withdraw consent at any time by:

Understanding Cookie Technology Internals

HTTP cookies consist primarily of a name-value pair set by the server or client-side scripts and stored in your browser. Cookies can be flagged with attributes controlling their expiration, path, domain, and security, influencing their persistence and availability to sites.

Secure cookies ensure transmission only occurs over encrypted HTTPS requests, guarding against eavesdropping. The HttpOnly flag restricts cookie access to server-side only, preventing client-side scripts from accessing sensitive cookies, offering protection against cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks.

SameSite attributes regulate cross-origin cookie sending, mitigating cross-site request forgery (CSRF) vulnerabilities by allowing cookies only for same-site interactions or strict conditions.

Browser privacy policies continue evolving, with many implementing default cookie restrictions or requiring explicit user consent. As web standards and protocols progress, the management and security of cookies remain vital for user privacy.

Recent Trends and Evolving Regulations

Privacy regulations worldwide are increasingly stringent, imposing requirements on cookie consent management, user rights, and data security.

Regulators have introduced cookie walls, stricter fines on consent violations, and standardizations to cookie handling for clarity and uniformity.

Technological shifts like browser anti-tracking initiatives and alternative privacy-preserving identification methods are shaping the future use of cookies and online tracking.

Ever Green Gardening actively monitors these trends and adapts our cookie policies and technologies accordingly.

Historical Context of Cookies

The cookie concept was introduced in the mid-1990s by web pioneers as a solution for stateless HTTP protocol limitations. Netscape Communications originally implemented cookies to handle shopping cart sessions, paving the way for modern web experiences.

Over the years, cookies evolved from simple state managers to complex tools used for identity, personalization, and advertising, sparking debates on privacy and regulation.

Privacy laws like the GDPR and ePrivacy Directive emerged to address these concerns, emphasizing transparency and user consent.

Technical Glossary

  • HTTP Cookie: Data sent from a website stored on a user's browser.
  • Session Cookie: Temporary cookie deleted when the browser closes.
  • Persistent Cookie: Cookie that remains for a set time for returning visits.
  • Third-Party Cookie: Cookie set by a domain other than the one visited.
  • SameSite Attribute: Controls cross-site cookie sending behavior.
  • HttpOnly Flag: Restricts cookie access to server only.
